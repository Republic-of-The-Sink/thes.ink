Quagsire

WARNING: COMPETITIVE POKÉMON AHEAD

At first glance, Quagsire might seem like a fairly unremarkable Pokémon. However, those of you that know a lot about Pokémon will know that Quagsire is far stronger than its pretty bad stat total implies. Quagsire gets the ability Unaware, which lets it ignore stat changes on other Pokémon. This makes it able to counter stat-boosted Pokémon that could otherwise sweep an entire team without issue.

Because of this, people tend to assume that Quagsire is a one-dimensional Pokémon that only exists to counter Zacian-Crowned and other Pokémon that rely on stat boosts. So what would happen if someone showed up to Anything Goes, the format where literally any Pokémon with any moveset is allowed, with a full team of Quagsire?

In Generation 8, this team was able to win a few times but didn't work particularly well as Quagsire's Water/Ground typing leaves it extremely vulnerable to Grass moves and Freeze Dry. In Generation 9 however, we got Tera Types, which lets you change the typing of one Pokémon. Now with the ability to remove Quagsire's singular weakness, it was now free to run around and take down any Pokémon.

Much rage ensued from Pokémon Showdown tryhards that lost to a random guy with 6 Quagsires while they were using useless terrible Pokémon like Miraidon or Calyrex-Shadow.
xad1561 has run the 6 Quagsire team in Pokémon Showdown many times before. The only format where this team is valid is Anything Goes, so it needs to be able to account for pretty much any and all Pokémon that can possibly show up. Much refinement of this team in battles means that it has a lot of seemingly random choices that are meant to counter specific Pokémon.

Some examples of seemingly random but actually important decisions include:
-Earth Power to counter suicide lead Glimmora
-Water Absorb on one Quagsire to stop Draco Meteor Miraidon from sweeping the team, because yes this is one of the few cases where Unaware is actually a detriment and yes this caused the team to get swept once
-One Quagsire with Tera-Dark+Tera Blast to kill Calyrex-Shadow, and two more that have Tera-Normal to stop it if that fails
-Covert Cloak to stop Garganacl from killing the entire team, and Waterfall over Liquidation to have 8 more PP to be able to PP stall it more effectively
-Focus Sash and Counter on two Quagsire, and Tera-Fire+Tera Blast on another to have a fighting chance against Grass-types
-Every Quagsire having no nickname and being set to always be male so that it's more difficult to tell the difference between each Quagsire from the opponent's perspective
